Pocket Shop — System Manual

For staff and administrators
v1.0

👤 User Roles

Every person who uses Pocket Shop has one role. The role decides what they can see and do in the app. Roles are set when a user registers and can only be changed by an admin.

🛍️

Buyer

  • Browse and search products
  • Add items to cart
  • Place and pay for orders
  • Track their deliveries
  • Rate products and sellers
  • Request cancellations
Cannot: list products, accept orders, do deliveries
🏪

Seller

  • List and manage products
  • Accept or reject incoming orders
  • Mark orders as preparing / ready
  • View their earnings
  • Request a payout withdrawal
Must complete Tier 1 verification before listing. Tier 2 unlocks higher limits.
🛵

Rider

  • Go online/offline
  • View and accept available deliveries
  • Scan QR codes at pickup and dropoff
  • Track their delivery earnings
Must complete Delivery verification before accepting jobs.
🧑‍💼

Staff

  • Process payout queue
  • Review verifications
  • Handle refunds
  • Send announcements
  • View system stats
Uses the staff app. Does NOT have Django admin access by default.
⚙️

Admin

  • Everything Staff can do
  • Full Django admin access
  • Edit any record directly
  • Manage platform settings
  • Change user roles
  • View all logs and tokens

How to change a user's role or deactivate them

  1. Go to Django Admin → Accounts → Users
  2. Search by phone number or name
  3. Click the user to open their record
  4. Change the Role field, or untick Is active to block login
  5. Click Save
Note: Deactivating a user immediately prevents them from logging in. Any active orders they have will not be cancelled automatically — you must handle those separately.

📊 The Staff Dashboard

The staff app home screen shows live numbers about what needs attention right now. Here is what every number means.

Payout Queue

How many payouts are ready to be sent out right now. These are sellers and riders who have completed their work — the QR scan happened, the money is cleared, and it is sitting waiting for staff to process it. This number should be kept as low as possible. If it is high, payouts are overdue.

Earnings Claims

Sellers or riders who have manually requested a withdrawal of their earned balance. Different from the payout queue — these are requests initiated by the user, not automatic post-delivery payouts.

Verifications

How many seller or rider verification applications are waiting for a staff member to review. A new seller or rider cannot operate until their verification is approved. If this number is high, users are waiting.

Refunds

Open refund requests that have not been processed yet. These are usually from cancelled orders or disputes.

Today's Revenue

Total money received from buyers today only, in ZMW. This only counts completed (confirmed) deposits — not pending payments. It resets at midnight.

Failed Payouts

Payouts that were sent but rejected by the mobile network — for example, because the phone number was wrong, the account was not registered for mobile money, or the network was down. These need to be investigated and retried.

Buyers / Sellers / Riders

A live count of all active registered users broken down by role. Useful for understanding the platform's growth at a glance.

📦 Order Statuses — Full Lifecycle

Every order goes through a series of statuses from the moment a buyer places it to when it is delivered. Here is what each one means, who triggers it, and what to do if an order is stuck there.

payment_pending → pending → accepted → preparing → out_for_delivery → delivered

An order can also move to cancelled from most statuses.

StatusTriggered byWhat it meansWho gets notifiedIf stuck here
payment_pending Buyer places order Order created. Waiting for MoMo payment to be confirmed. Money has not arrived yet. Buyer gets a "Awaiting payment" notification Payment failed silently. Tell the buyer to check their MoMo app and retry. If it has been more than 10 minutes, the payment likely failed.
pending PawaPay webhook (payment confirmed) Money received and held. Seller is notified and must now review and accept the order. Seller gets "New Order" push notification. Buyer gets "Order submitted" notification. Seller has not opened the app or has not responded. Contact the seller. If they cannot fulfil, cancel the order and issue a refund.
accepted Seller taps Accept in the app Seller has committed to fulfil this order. Buyer gets "Order Accepted" notification Seller accepted but is not preparing. Follow up with them.
preparing Seller taps Preparing Order is actively being packed or made. Buyer gets "Order Being Prepared" notification Seller has not progressed for a long time. Follow up.
out_for_delivery Seller marks ready Order is packed and waiting for a rider to accept it and come to collect. Buyer gets "Out for Delivery" notification No rider has accepted. Check that riders are online in the area. The order stays here until a rider picks it up.
delivered Rider scans buyer QR and taps "Mark as Delivered" Order complete. Payouts are queued for seller and rider. Buyer and Seller both get "Order Delivered" notification This is a final state. Nothing to do.
cancelled Seller or buyer cancels, or staff cancels Order stopped. If payment was made, a refund should be issued. Buyer and Seller both get "Order Cancelled" notification (only if payment was made) Check if a refund has been created. If not, create one manually — see the Refund guide below.

🛵 Delivery Assignment Statuses

Once a rider accepts an order, a delivery assignment is created. It has its own separate set of statuses that track where the rider is in the delivery process.

accepted → picked_up → in_transit → delivered
StatusTriggered byWhat it meansIf stuck here
accepted Rider taps Accept on an order card Rider is heading to the seller's location to collect the order. Rider accepted but has not moved. Contact the rider. If they are not responding, the assignment may need to be cancelled and re-offered.
picked_up Rider scans the seller's QR code at pickup Seller confirmed the handoff. The order is now physically in the rider's hands. Pickup scan happened but rider is not moving. Contact the rider.
in_transit System sets this automatically after pickup scan Rider is on the way to the buyer's address. Rider has been in transit for an unusually long time. Contact them and the buyer.
delivered Rider scans buyer QR and confirms Buyer confirmed receipt. Delivery is complete. Final state. Nothing to do.

💰 Payout Stages — Where the Money Is

When a buyer pays, the money does not immediately go to the seller or rider. It is held and released in stages as work is confirmed. This protects everyone.

There are two separate payout rows created for every delivered order: one for the seller, and one for the rider.

Think of it like an envelope system. When payment is confirmed, the money goes into two envelopes — one for the seller, one for the rider. Each envelope is only opened and handed over once the relevant QR is scanned to prove the work was done.
StageWhose payoutTriggered byWhat it means
pickup_pending_scan Seller Payment confirmed Seller's money is locked and waiting. The pickup QR scan has not happened yet.
ready_for_payout Seller Rider scans seller's QR at pickup Seller has proven they handed the order to the rider. Their money is now cleared and ready. Staff should process this from the payout queue.
dropoff_pending_scan Rider Payment confirmed Rider's delivery fee is locked and waiting. The dropoff QR scan has not happened yet.
ready_for_payout Rider Rider scans buyer's QR at dropoff Buyer confirmed receipt. Rider's fee is cleared and ready to send.
payout_initiated Both Staff processes payout from queue The payout request has been sent to PawaPay. Waiting for the network to confirm.
payout_paid Both PawaPay sends webhook confirmation Money has arrived in the recipient's mobile wallet. Payout is complete.
Pending earnings explained: In the seller or rider's earnings screen, "pending" only shows payouts at ready_for_payout or later — not the early locked stages. This means the number they see is money that has actually been earned and confirmed, not just promised.

💳 Transaction / Payment Statuses

Every movement of money — whether a buyer paying in, a payout going out, or a refund — is recorded as a Transaction. Transactions have their own status.

StatusWhat it meansWhat to do
pending Waiting for confirmation from the mobile network. The prompt has been sent to the user's phone but they have not approved it yet. Wait. It will resolve to completed, failed, or cancelled. If it has been more than 10 minutes, it likely failed silently.
completed Confirmed and settled. Money has moved. Nothing to do.
failed The payment network rejected it. The failure message field will say why. Read the failure message. Common causes: insufficient balance, wrong number, network error. For deposits: tell the buyer to retry. For payouts: fix the number and retry.
cancelled The user saw the MoMo prompt on their phone and declined it. For deposits: the buyer changed their mind. The order goes back to payment_pending. For payouts: unusual — contact the recipient.
terminated The payment timed out — the user did not respond to the MoMo prompt before it expired. Same as failed — retry.

✅ Verification Statuses

Sellers and riders must be verified before they can operate. Sellers need Tier 1 (and optionally Tier 2) verification. Riders need Delivery verification. Staff review submitted applications and approve or reject them.

StatusWhat it meansWhat staff see
not_started User registered but has not submitted any documents yet. Nothing to action — they have not applied.
submitted User has uploaded their documents and is waiting for review. They appear in the staff Verifications queue. Documents to review. Must approve or reject.
approved Staff reviewed and approved. The seller can now list products / the rider can now accept deliveries. No action needed. User is active.
rejected Staff rejected the application. The rejection reason is shown to the user in the app so they know what to fix and resubmit. User may resubmit — will appear in queue again when they do.
Verification types: seller_tier1 — basic seller, required to list products. seller_tier2 — advanced tier, higher limits. delivery — rider verification, requires license photos and a live selfie.

📱 QR Scan Flow

The QR system is the proof-of-work mechanism. It makes sure money only moves when the right person confirmed the right handoff. Nobody can fake a scan — each QR is unique to the user and expires.

The two QR checks

1. Pickup QR — Seller's QR

Who shows it: The seller opens the app and shows their identity QR.
Who scans it: The rider, when they arrive at the seller's location to collect the order.
What it proves: This specific rider collected from this specific seller. Not a stranger, not someone else's rider.
What it triggers: Order moves to picked_up. Seller's payout moves to ready_for_payout.

2. Dropoff QR — Buyer's QR

Who shows it: The buyer opens the app and shows their identity QR when the rider arrives.
Who scans it: The rider, at the buyer's door.
What it proves: This specific buyer received this specific order from this specific rider.
What it triggers: Rider taps "Mark as Delivered". Order moves to delivered. Rider's payout moves to ready_for_payout.

What to do if a QR scan fails

  • The QR code expires after a set time. If too much time passed, the user must regenerate it (re-open their QR screen in the app).
  • If the rider cannot get the QR to scan, check that the rider is on the correct order and the buyer is showing the right screen.
  • In a genuine emergency (e.g. buyer has no phone battery), an admin can manually advance the order status from Django Admin → Orders. You will also need to manually move the payout stage in Payments → Transactions.
Never skip the QR. The QR scan is the only proof that the handoff happened. If you manually advance without it, you are releasing money without confirmation. Only do this if you have spoken to both parties and are certain the delivery occurred.

🗂️ Admin Panel — Accounts

Users

The master list of everyone registered on the platform. This is where you look up any user, change their role, or deactivate them.

  • Phone numberTheir login. Always stored in international format, e.g. +260974086484. This is how users are identified — not by name.

🗂️ Admin Panel — Payments & Transactions

This is the most important section for staff. Every movement of money is recorded here.

Transaction Types

TypeDirectionWhat it represents
DepositBuyer → PlatformA buyer paying for an order via MoMo. The money comes in and is held until the order is fulfilled.
PayoutPlatform → Seller or RiderSending earned money out to a seller (for selling) or rider (for delivering).
RefundPlatform → BuyerReturning money to a buyer after a cancellation or dispute.

Every field on a Transaction record

  • OrderWhich order this money belongs to. Click it to jump to the order record.
  • Transaction typeDeposit, Payout, or Refund — see table above.
  • AmountHow much money, in ZMW.
  • CurrencyAlways ZMW (Zambian Kwacha) for now.
  • ProviderWhich mobile network: MTN_MOMO_ZMB, AIRTEL_OAPI_ZMB, or ZAMTEL_MONEY_ZMB. Determined by the phone number prefix.
  • Payer numberFor deposits: the buyer's number money came FROM. For payouts/refunds: the number money is going TO.
  • RecipientFor payouts: the seller or rider user account that will receive the money. Empty for deposits.
  • Recipient role"seller" or "delivery" — for payouts only. Determines which earnings bucket the payment goes into.
  • Payout stageWhere this payout is in the pipeline. Only relevant for payout transactions — see Payout Stages section above. For deposits this shows "Not Applicable".
  • Trigger eventManual = a staff member or user created this; pickup_qr = the pickup scan triggered it; dropoff_qr = the dropoff scan triggered it.
  • Payout methodManual = staff physically sends from the platform's own phone. Auto = system sends via PawaPay API automatically.
  • Failure messageIf the transaction failed, PawaPay's error reason is recorded here. Read this to understand what went wrong.
  • Marked paid byIf staff manually confirmed the payment, their username is recorded here.
  • Marked paid atThe date and time the manual confirmation was made. Note: the server is 2 hours behind your local time — adjust when reading timestamps.
  • Payout notesA free-text notes field for staff. Use it to record what you did — e.g. "Called recipient, confirmed received on 20 June at 14:00".
  • Statuspending / completed / failed / cancelled / terminated — see Transaction Statuses section.
  • Transaction IDA unique UUID like 5dba45c0-8fba-407a-a397-d5eefdaeb3b8. Give this to PawaPay support when reporting an issue with a specific payment.
  • Created at / Updated atWhen the transaction was created and last modified.
Server time note: The admin panel says "You are 2 hours ahead of server time." This means if you set "Marked paid at" to now, the server will record it as 2 hours earlier. Always check this note when reading timestamps — add 2 hours to get your local time.

📋 How to Process a Payout

A payout is when you send earned money to a seller or rider. This should be done as soon as items appear in the payout queue.

Open the Staff App and check the Payout Queue number.

If it shows a number greater than zero, there are payouts waiting. Tap the Payout Queue card.

Review each payout in the list.

For each one you will see: the order number, the recipient's name and phone, the amount, and the currency. Confirm the phone number looks correct before sending.

Send the money.

If the payout method is Manual, you physically send the money from the platform's mobile money account to the recipient's number. If it is Auto, tap "Process" and the system sends it via PawaPay.

Mark it as paid.

In the admin panel (Payments → Transactions), find the transaction, set Status to Completed, set Marked paid by to your username, set Marked paid at to now, and write a note in Payout notes. Save.

Confirm with the recipient (optional but recommended).

For large payouts, call or message the recipient to confirm they received it. Note the confirmation in Payout notes.

If a payout fails: Read the Failure message on the transaction. Common causes: wrong number, unregistered MoMo account, network error. Contact the recipient to verify their number, correct it in the transaction record, and retry. Never mark a failed payout as completed.

📋 How to Do a Manual Refund

A refund returns money to a buyer. This is needed when an order is cancelled after payment was confirmed, or after a dispute is resolved in the buyer's favour.

When to issue a refund

  • Order was cancelled and the buyer had already paid
  • Order was delivered but items were wrong or missing and buyer has a valid complaint
  • Duplicate charge — buyer was charged twice
  • Rider collected but never delivered and the order cannot be re-delivered

When NOT to refund without investigation

  • Buyer says they did not receive the order but QR scan confirms delivery happened — investigate first
  • Buyer changed their mind after the seller has already accepted and started preparing
Find the original deposit transaction.

Go to Admin Panel → Payments → Transactions. Filter by transaction type = Deposit and search for the order number or buyer's phone number. Find the completed deposit for the order in question.

Note the buyer's phone number and the amount paid.

The payer number on the deposit is who you are refunding to. The amount is what you are refunding (full or partial).

Create a new Refund transaction.

In Admin → Payments → Transactions → Add Transaction. Set: Transaction type = Refund, Order = the same order, Amount = refund amount, Payer number = buyer's phone, Status = Pending, Payout method = Manual or Auto. Save.

Send the refund.

If manual: send money from the platform account to the buyer's number. If auto: trigger via PawaPay.

Update the refund transaction to Completed.

Set Status = Completed, fill in Marked paid by and Marked paid at, add a note explaining the reason for the refund. Save.

Update the order status if needed.

If the order is still showing as pending or accepted, set it to Cancelled in Admin → Orders so it no longer appears in seller and rider queues.

Partial refund: If you are only refunding part of the amount (e.g. one item was missing but others were delivered), enter the partial amount on the refund transaction and note the reason clearly in Payout notes.
What the buyer sees: After you process the refund, the buyer gets a push notification saying their refund has been processed. They will see it arrive in their MoMo wallet within minutes (for MTN and Airtel) or up to a few hours (Zamtel).

📋 How to Review a Verification

Open the Verifications queue in the staff app.

It shows all submitted applications waiting for review. Tap one to open it.

Check each document carefully.

For sellers: check their shop details are complete and real.
For riders: check all four of these:

  • License front image — must be clear, all text readable, not expired
  • License back image — same standards
  • Live verification photo — must be a clear selfie of a real person, not a photo of a photo
  • Profile photo — optional but helpful
Approve or reject.

If everything looks good: tap Approve. The user is immediately able to start operating.
If something is wrong: tap Reject. You must write a clear rejection reason. This is shown directly to the user — be specific about what they need to fix.

The user is notified automatically.

They receive a push notification telling them the result. If rejected, they can fix the issue and resubmit — it will appear in your queue again.

Good rejection reason examples

  • "License photo is too dark — please retake in good lighting with all text clearly visible"
  • "License back image is missing — please upload a photo of the back of your license"
  • "Live verification photo does not show your face clearly — please take a front-facing selfie"
  • "License appears to be expired — please upload a valid current license"

📋 How to Send an Announcement

Go to Admin Panel → Notifications → Announcements → Add.
Write your title and message.

Keep the title short (under 60 characters). The message should be clear and direct.

Set the target audience.

All = everyone. Buyers only = people who shop. Sellers only = shop owners. Delivery = riders only.

Save. The announcement sends automatically.

Once saved, push notifications go out to all targeted users immediately. The in-app notification also appears in their notification list.

You cannot unsend an announcement. Once saved, it is delivered. Double-check your message and audience before saving.

📋 How to Deactivate a User

Go to Admin Panel → Accounts → Users.
Search by phone number and open the user record.
Untick "Is active" and save.

The user is immediately logged out on all devices and cannot log back in.

Handle any open orders.

Check if they have any active orders. Cancel them manually and issue refunds if needed.

To re-activate, tick "Is active" again and save. Their data is preserved.

📋 How to Look Up an Order or Transaction

When a user contacts you about a problem, here is the fastest way to find what you need.

To find an order

  1. Admin → Orders → Orders
  2. Search by the order number (e.g. ORD9D4F0BC6) or filter by buyer/seller phone
  3. Click the order to see full details including items, status, and delivery

To find a payment

  1. Admin → Payments → Transactions
  2. Search by the Transaction ID (UUID) if you have it, or filter by payer number / order
  3. If the user says "I paid but nothing happened", filter by payer number and sort by created date — look for a pending or failed deposit

To find a user

  1. Admin → Accounts → Users
  2. Search by phone number (include the country code: +260)

🔧 Common Problems and Fixes

  • 🔴 Buyer says they paid but the order is still showing "payment pending"
    Check: Admin → Payments → Transactions. Filter by the buyer's phone number. If there is a failed or cancelled deposit, the payment did not go through. Tell the buyer to retry. If there is a completed deposit but the order is still payment_pending, there may be a webhook issue — contact technical support.
  • 🔴 Seller says they never received a notification for a new order
    Check: Admin → Accounts → Users → find the seller → check if their FCM token field is filled. If blank, they have not logged in recently and their notification address is missing — ask them to log out and back in on their device.
  • 🔴 Order is stuck at "out_for_delivery" — no rider is picking it up
    Check: Are any riders online? Check Admin → Accounts → Delivery Profiles → filter by Is available = true. If no riders are online in the area, contact riders to come online. If the issue persists, the order may need to be manually re-offered.
  • 🔴 Payout failed — what do I do?
    Check: The Failure message field on the transaction. Most common causes:
    • "Invalid recipient number" — wrong or unregistered number. Contact the recipient to confirm their MoMo-registered number, update the Payer number field, and retry.
    • "Insufficient funds in platform account" — the platform's MoMo account is low. Top it up and retry.
    • "Network error" — temporary network issue. Wait a few minutes and retry.
  • 🔴 Rider cannot scan the QR code
    The token may have expired. Ask the seller or buyer to close and re-open their QR screen in the app to regenerate it. If the problem persists, check Admin → Delivery → Delivery assignments for that order — the assignment must be in the correct status for the scan to be accepted.
  • 🔴 User says they are locked out / cannot log in
    Check Admin → Accounts → Users → find the user → confirm "Is active" is ticked. If it is ticked but they still cannot log in, check Admin → Token Blacklist → Outstanding tokens to see if their session was invalidated. Ask them to use OTP login to get a fresh token.
  • 🔴 Rider's earnings show as zero even after completing deliveries
    Check Admin → Payments → Transactions → filter by recipient = that rider. If payout rows exist but are at pickup_pending_scan or dropoff_pending_scan, the QR was not scanned. If the delivery genuinely happened, you can manually advance the payout stage to ready_for_payout and then process the payout.
  • 🔴 Buyer received wrong items — wants a refund
    Investigate: confirm the order items against what was delivered. If the seller's fault: issue a refund to the buyer (see Refund guide), and contact the seller. The seller's payout for that order should be held until resolved. If disputed: get evidence from both sides before deciding.

📖 Glossary

PawaPay
The payment processing company that handles all mobile money transactions on the platform. They connect to MTN, Airtel, and Zamtel networks.
MoMo / Mobile Money
A way to send and receive money using a mobile phone number — like MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, or Zamtel Kwacha.
Webhook
An automatic message sent by PawaPay to the platform when a payment status changes. For example, when a buyer's MoMo payment goes through, PawaPay sends a webhook to tell the system — the order then moves from payment_pending to pending.
FCM Token
A unique address for a specific app installation on a specific phone. The system uses it to send push notifications. It changes if the user reinstalls the app.
QR Secret
A private key stored on each user's account. Used to generate their identity QR code. Never share this with anyone.
Escrow
Money that is held safely by a neutral party until certain conditions are met. On Pocket Shop, buyer payments are held in escrow until the order is delivered — then released to seller and rider.
Payout Stage
The current step in the process of releasing held money to a seller or rider. Moves from locked → confirmed → sent → received.
Delivery Assignment
The record created when a rider accepts a delivery. Tracks the rider's progress from pickup to dropoff.
Handoff
The physical act of exchanging the order — either from seller to rider (pickup handoff) or from rider to buyer (dropoff handoff). Both are confirmed by QR scan.
UUID
A long unique ID like "5dba45c0-8fba-407a-a397-d5eefdaeb3b8". Used as transaction IDs and other system references. Paste these when contacting support.
Tier 1 / Tier 2 (Seller)
Verification levels for sellers. Tier 1 is required to list any products. Tier 2 unlocks higher product limits and additional features.
Sandbox
A test mode for payments where no real money moves. Used during development. The platform should always be in Live mode in production.
ETA
Estimated Time of Arrival — how long the system thinks a delivery will take, calculated from the rider's location and speed profile.
E.164
The international phone number format used in the system. Zambian numbers look like +260974086484.
